style="display:none">/n<td colspan="2"></td>/n</tr>/n</table>/n<p><b>Mads Tofte</b> (born 20 April 1959) is a <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> <a href="/wiki/Computer_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer Science">computer scientist</a> who has contributed in particular to <a href="/wiki/Functional_programming" title="Functional programming">functional class="toctitle">/n<h2>Contents</h2>/n</div>/n<ul>/n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Education"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Education</span></a></li>/n<li links</span></a></li>/n</ul>/n</div>/n<p></p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mads_Tofte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<p>Tofte was born in <a href="/wiki/Kongens_Lyngby" title="Kongens Lyngby">Lyngby</a>, Denmark and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Holb%C3%A6k" title="Holbæk">Holbæk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>. He studied <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Copenhagen" title="University of Copenhagen">University of Copenhagen</a> where he obtained an <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">MSc</a> degree (with supervisor <a href="/wiki/Neil_D._Jones" title="Neil D. Jones">Neil D. Jones</a>) in 1984; then at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">University of Edinburgh</a> where he obtained a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">PhD</a> degree in 1988 (advised by <a href="/wiki/Robin_Milner" title="Robin Milner">Robin Milner</a>). He is doctor honoris causa 2007 from <a href="/wiki/Kingston_University" title="Kingston University">Kingston University</a>.</p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_and_career">Research and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mads_Tofte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Research and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<p>In his 1984 <a href="/wiki/MSc" class="mw-redirect" title="MSc">MSc</a> thesis<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and prior work he investigated and formalized the CERES compiler generator (with Neil D. Jones), and showed that (1) a compiler generator is itself a compiler from language definitions to compilers; and (2) under suitable assumptions there exists a language definition that, when applied to itself, generates a compiler generator. This has close connections to self-application in <a href="/wiki/Partial_evaluation" title="Partial evaluation">partial evaluation</a>.</p>/n<p>In his <a href="/wiki/PhD" class="mw-redirect" title="PhD">PhD</a> thesis he developed and proved correct the first sound type system for <a href="/wiki/ML_(programming_language)" title="ML (programming language)">ML</a>-style <a href="/wiki/Type_polymorphism" class="mw-redirect" title="Type polymorphism">polymorphic references</a>, an important open problem at the time. Moreover, he formalized a variant of the module system of the <a href="/wiki/Standard_ML" title="Standard ML">Standard ML</a> programming language.</p>/n<p>Mads Tofte is a co-author of the Definition<sup id="cite_ref-definition_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-definition-2">[2]</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Standard_ML" title="Standard ML">Standard ML</a> and the associated Commentary, probably the most precise description developed for any realistic programming language. He co-developed the ML Kit, an implementation of <a href="/wiki/Standard_ML" title="Standard ML">Standard ML</a> whose structure closely follows the Definition.</p>/n<p>Subsequently he developed (with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jean-Pierre_Talpin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jean-Pierre Talpin (page does not exist)">Jean-Pierre Talpin</a>) the notion of <a href="/wiki/Region_inference" class="mw-redirect" title="Region inference">region inference</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Program_analysis_(computer_science)" class="mw-redirect" title="Program analysis (computer science)">program analysis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Memory_management" title="Memory management">memory management</a> technique that avoids or minimizes the use of <a href="/wiki/Garbage_(computer_science)" title="Garbage (computer science)">garbage collection</a>. This work was first published<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/POPL" class="mw-redirect" title="POPL">POPL</a> 1994 and in 2005 it earned the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a> (ACM) <a href="/wiki/POPL" class="mw-redirect" title="POPL">POPL</a> 1994 Most Influential Paper Award.</p>/n<p>In the late nineties he co-developed (with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fritz_Henglein&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fritz Henglein (page does not exist)">Fritz Henglein</a> and others) a type system and a sophisticated tool called AnnoDomini for mitigation of the <a href="/wiki/Year_2000_problem" title="Year 2000 problem">Year 2000 problem</a> in <a href="/wiki/COBOL" title="COBOL">COBOL</a> software. The tool analyses legacy programs to discover all data fields that are used as dates. This work was presented in a POPL 1999 invited keynote.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>/n<p>In April 1999 he was appointed the first managing director of the <a href="/wiki/IT_University_of_Copenhagen" title="IT University of Copenhagen">IT University of Copenhagen</a>. He oversaw the creation of the university from scratch, the hiring of faculty/staff, recruitment of students and the design of the study programs. The first students started 5 months later in September 1999. Since 2003 he has been <a href="/wiki/Vice_chancellor" class="mw-redirect" title="Vice chancellor">vice chancellor</a> of the <a href="/wiki/IT_University_of_Copenhagen" title="IT University of Copenhagen">IT University of Copenhagen</a>.</p>/n<p><br /></p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mads_Tofte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<ul>/n<li>2002 The IDG IT award (<i>IT-prisen</i>), for leadership of the <a href="/wiki/IT_University_of_Copenhagen" title="IT University of Copenhagen">IT University of Copenhagen</a></li>/n<li>2005 The <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">ACM</a> <a href="/wiki/POPL" class="mw-redirect" title="POPL">POPL</a> '94 Most Influential Paper Award (with Jean-Pierre Talpin).</li>/n</ul>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mads_Tofte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<div class="mw-references-wrap">/n<ol class="references">/n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">M.
