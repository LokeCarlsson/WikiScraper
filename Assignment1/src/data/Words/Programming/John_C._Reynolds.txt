href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>/n</ul>/n</div>/n<p></p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_affiliations">Education and affiliations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_C._Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education and affiliations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<p>John Reynolds studied at <a href="/wiki/Purdue_University" title="Purdue University">Purdue University</a> and then earned a <a href="/wiki/PhD" class="mw-redirect" title="PhD">PhD</a> in <a href="/wiki/Theoretical_physics" title="Theoretical physics">theoretical physics</a> from <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> in 1961. He was Professor of <a href="/wiki/Information_science" title="Information science">Information science</a> at <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_University" title="Syracuse University">Syracuse University</a> from 1970 to 1986. From then until his death he was Professor of <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">Computer Science</a> at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University" title="Carnegie Mellon University">Carnegie Mellon University</a>. He also held visiting positions at <a href="/wiki/Aarhus_University" title="Aarhus University">Aarhus University</a> (<a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>), <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">University of Edinburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Imperial_College_London" title="Imperial College London">Imperial College London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Research" title="Microsoft Research">Microsoft Research</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Queen_Mary,_University_of_London" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen Mary, University of London">Queen Mary, University of London</a> (<a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a>).</p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_work">Academic work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_C._Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academic work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<p>Reynolds's main research interest was in the area of <a href="/wiki/Programming_language" title="Programming language">programming language</a> design and associated <a href="/wiki/Specification_language" title="Specification language">specification languages</a>, especially concerning formal <a href="/wiki/Semantics" title="Semantics">semantics</a>. He invented the <a href="/wiki/Polymorphic_lambda_calculus" class="mw-redirect" title="Polymorphic lambda calculus">polymorphic lambda calculus</a> (System F) and formulated the property of semantic <a href="/wiki/Parametricity" title="Parametricity">parametricity</a>; the same calculus was independently discovered by <a href="/wiki/Jean-Yves_Girard" title="Jean-Yves Girard">Jean-Yves Girard</a>. He wrote a seminal paper on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Definitional_interpreters&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Definitional interpreters (page does not exist)">definitional interpreters</a>, which clarified early work on <a href="/wiki/Continuations" class="mw-redirect" title="Continuations">continuations</a> and introduced the technique of <a href="/wiki/Defunctionalization" title="Defunctionalization">defunctionalization</a>. He applied <a href="/wiki/Category_theory" title="Category theory">category theory</a> to <a href="/wiki/Programming_language_semantics" class="mw-redirect" title="Programming language semantics">programming language semantics</a>. He defined the programming languages <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gedanken_programming_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gedanken programming language (page does not exist)">Gedanken</a> and <a href="/wiki/Forsythe_(programming_language)" class="mw-redirect" title="Forsythe (programming language)">Forsythe</a>, known for its use of <a href="/wiki/Intersection_type" class="mw-redirect" title="Intersection type">intersection types</a>. He worked on a <a href="/wiki/Separation_logic" title="Separation logic">separation logic</a> to describe and reason about shared mutable <a href="/wiki/Data_structure" title="Data structure">data structures</a>.</p>/n<p>He had been an editor of journals such as the <i><a href="/wiki/Communications_of_the_ACM" title="Communications of the ACM">Communications of the ACM</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_of_the_ACM" title="Journal of the ACM">Journal of the ACM</a></i>. In 2001, he was appointed a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">ACM</a>. He won the <a href="/wiki/SIGPLAN#Awards" title="SIGPLAN">ACM SIGPLAN Programming Language Achievement Award</a> in 2003, and the <a href="/wiki/Lovelace_Medal" title="Lovelace Medal">Lovelace Medal</a> from the <a href="/wiki/British_Computer_Society" title="British Computer Society">British Computer Society</a> in 2010.</p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_publications">Selected publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_C._Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Selected publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<dl>/n<dt>Books</dt>/n</dl>/n<ul>/n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://repository.cmu.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=2279&amp;context=compsci"><i>The Craft of Programming</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Prentice_Hall" title="Prentice Hall">Prentice Hall</a> International, 1981. <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Book_Number" title="International Standard Book Number">ISBN</a>&#160;<a href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0-13-188862-5" title="Special:BookSources/0-13-188862-5">0-13-188862-5</a>.</li>/n<li><i>Theories of Programming Languages</i>, <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_University_Press" title="Cambridge University Press">Cambridge University Press</a>, 1998. <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Book_Number" title="International Standard Book Number">ISBN</a>&#160;<a href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0-521-59414-6" title="Special:BookSources/0-521-59414-6">0-521-59414-6</a>.</li>/n</ul>/n<dl>/n<dt>Articles</dt>/n</dl>/n<ul>/n<li><cite class="citation journal"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cs.cmu.edu/afs/cs/user/jcr/ftp/transysalg.pdf">"Transformational Systems and the Algebraic Structure of Atomic Formulas"</a> <span style="font-size:85%;">(PDF)</span>. <i>Machine Intelligence</i>.
