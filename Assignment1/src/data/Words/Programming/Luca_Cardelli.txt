colspan="2"></td>/n</tr>/n</table>/n<p><b>Luca Andrea Cardelli</b> <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">FRS</a> is an Italian <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> who is an Assistant Director at <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Research" title="Microsoft Research">Microsoft Research</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_UK" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambridge, UK">Cambridge, UK</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-whoswho_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whoswho-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Cardelli is well known for his research in <a href="/wiki/Type_theory" title="Type theory">type theory</a> and <a href="/wiki/Operational_semantics" title="Operational semantics">operational semantics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-onunderstanding_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-onunderstanding-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-badprop_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-badprop-7">[7]</a></sup> Among other contributions, he helped design <a href="/wiki/Modula-3" title="Modula-3">Modula-3</a>, implemented the first compiler for the (non-pure) <a href="/wiki/Functional_programming" title="Functional programming">functional</a> <a href="/wiki/Programming_language" title="Programming language">programming language</a> <a href="/wiki/ML_programming_language" class="mw-redirect" title="ML programming language">ML</a>, and defined the concept of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Typeful_programming&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Typeful programming (page does not exist)">typeful programming</a>. He helped develop the <a href="/wiki/Polyphonic_C_sharp" class="mw-redirect" title="Polyphonic C sharp">Polyphonic C#</a> experimental programming language.<sup id="cite_ref-theoryofobjects_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theoryofobjects-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-acm_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acm-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dblp_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dblp-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-microsoft_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-microsoft-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-scopus_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scopus-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>/n<p></p>/n<div id="toc" class="toc">/n<div class="toctitle">/n<h2>Contents</h2>/n</div>/n<ul>/n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Education"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Education</span></a></li>/n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Awards"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Awards</span></a></li>/n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>/n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>/n</ul>/n</div>/n<p></p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Cardelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<p>He was born in <a href="/wiki/Montecatini_Terme" title="Montecatini Terme">Montecatini Terme</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. He attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pisa" title="University of Pisa">University of Pisa</a><sup id="cite_ref-whoswho_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whoswho-1">[1]</a></sup> before receiving his <a href="/wiki/PhD" class="mw-redirect" title="PhD">PhD</a><sup id="cite_ref-cardelliphd_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cardelliphd-13">[13]</a></sup> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">University of Edinburgh</a> in 1982. Before joining Microsoft Research in 1997, he worked for <a href="/wiki/Bell_Labs" title="Bell Labs">Bell Labs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Digital_Equipment_Corporation" title="Digital Equipment Corporation">Digital Equipment Corporation</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-whoswho_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whoswho-1">[1]</a></sup> and contributed to <a href="/wiki/Unix" title="Unix">Unix</a> software including <a href="/wiki/Vismon" title="Vismon">vismon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-reader_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reader-14">[14]</a></sup></p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Cardelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<p>In 2004 he was inducted as a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a>. He is a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a>.
