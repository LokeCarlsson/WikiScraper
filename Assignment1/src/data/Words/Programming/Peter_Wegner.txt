href="#cite_note-mathgene-3">[3]</a></sup></td>/n</tr>/n<tr style="display:none">/n<td colspan="2"></td>/n</tr>/n</table>/n<p><b>Peter A.<sup id="cite_ref-Warren_2013_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Warren_2013-4">[4]</a></sup> Wegner</b> (August 20, 1932 – July 27, 2017) was a computer scientist who made significant contributions to both the theory of <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_programming" title="Object-oriented programming">object-oriented programming</a> during the 1980s and to the relevance of the <a href="/wiki/Church%E2%80%93Turing_thesis" title="Church–Turing thesis">Church–Turing thesis</a> for empirical aspects of <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> during the 1990s and present. In 2016, Wegner wrote a brief autobiography for <i>Conduit</i>, the annual <a href="/wiki/Brown_University" title="Brown University">Brown University</a> Computer Science department magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MathSciNet_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MathSciNet-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dblp_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dblp-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-microsoft_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-microsoft-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>/n<p></p>/n<div id="toc" class="toc">/n<div class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peter_Wegner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<p>Wegner was educated at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a> and received a Post-Graduate Diploma in Numerical Analysis and Automatic Computing in 1954, at a time when there were no <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">PhD</a> programs in computer science.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup> He was awarded a PhD from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a> in 1968 for his book <i>Programming Languages, Information Structures, and Machine Organization</i>, with <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Wilkes" title="Maurice Wilkes">Maurice Wilkes</a> listed as his supervisor.<sup id="cite_ref-wegnerphd_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wegnerphd-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mathgene_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mathgene-3">[3]</a></sup></p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peter_Wegner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<p>The seminal work for his previous occupation<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (November 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> is <i>On Understanding Types</i><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> which was co-authored with <a href="/wiki/Luca_Cardelli" title="Luca Cardelli">Luca Cardelli</a>. For his latter undertaking, he co-authored several papers and co-edited a book <i>Interactive Computation: the New Paradigm</i> which was published in 2006.</p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peter_Wegner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<p>Wegner was inducted as a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a> (ACM) in 1995 and received the ACM Distinguished Service Award in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 1999, he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Decoration_for_Science_and_Art#Divisions" title="Austrian Decoration for Science and Art">Austrian Cross of Honor for Science and Art, 1st class</a> ("Österreichisches Ehrenkreuz für Wissenschaft u. Kunst I. Klasse"),<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> but was hit by a bus and sustained serious brain injuries when on a trip to London to receive his award.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He recovered after a lengthy coma.</p>/n<p>He was the editor-in-chief of <a href="/wiki/ACM_Computing_Surveys" title="ACM Computing Surveys">ACM Computing Surveys</a><sup id="cite_ref-acmdistgsvc_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acmdistgsvc-16">[16]</a></sup> and of The Brown Faculty Bulletin.
