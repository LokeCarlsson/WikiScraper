science">computer science</a> department of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a>. As of 2007, it employs 35 academic staff, 25 support staff, 35 affiliated research staff, and about 155 research students. The current head of department is Professor <a href="/wiki/Andy_Hopper" title="Andy Hopper">Andy Hopper</a>.</p>/n<p></p>/n<div id="toc" class="toc">/n<div class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_Laboratory,_University_of_Cambridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<p>The <b>Cambridge University Computer Laboratory</b> was founded as the <i>Mathematical Laboratory</i> under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/John_Lennard-Jones" title="John Lennard-Jones">John Lennard-Jones</a> on 14 May 1937, though it did not get properly established until after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. The new laboratory was housed in the North Wing of the former Anatomy School, on the <a href="/wiki/New_Museums_Site" title="New Museums Site">New Museums Site</a>. Upon its foundation, it was intended <i>to provide a computing service for general use, and to be a centre for the development of computational techniques in the University</i>. The <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Diploma_in_Computer_Science" title="Cambridge Diploma in Computer Science">Cambridge Diploma in Computer Science</a> was the world’s first postgraduate taught course in computing, starting in 1953.</p>/n<p>In October 1946, work began under <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Wilkes" title="Maurice Wilkes">Maurice Wilkes</a> on <a href="/wiki/EDSAC" class="mw-redirect" title="EDSAC">EDSAC</a> (<i>Electronic Delay Storage Automatic Calculator</i>), which subsequently became the world’s first fully operational and practical <a href="/wiki/Stored_program" class="mw-redirect" title="Stored program">stored program</a> computer when it ran its first program on 6 May 1949. It inspired the world’s first business computer, <a href="/wiki/LEO_computer" class="mw-redirect" title="LEO computer">LEO</a>. It was replaced by <a href="/wiki/EDSAC_2" title="EDSAC 2">EDSAC 2</a>, the first microcoded and bitsliced computer, in 1958.</p>/n<p>In 1961, <a href="/wiki/David_Hartley_(computer_scientist)" title="David Hartley (computer scientist)">David Hartley</a> developed <a href="/wiki/Autocode" title="Autocode">Autocode</a>, one of the first <a href="/wiki/High-level_programming_language" title="High-level programming language">high-level programming languages</a>, for <a href="/wiki/EDSAC_2" title="EDSAC 2">EDSAC 2</a>. Also in that year, proposals for <a href="/wiki/Titan_(computer)" title="Titan (computer)">Titan</a>, based on the <a href="/wiki/Atlas_Computer_(Manchester)" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlas Computer (Manchester)">Ferranti Atlas</a> machine, were developed. Titan became fully operational in 1964 and EDSAC 2 was retired the following year. In 1967, a full (‘24/7’) multi-user time-shared service for up to 64 users was inaugurated on Titan.</p>/n<p>In 1970, the Mathematical Laboratory was renamed the Computer Laboratory, with separate departments for Teaching and Research and the Computing Service, providing computing services to the university and its colleges. The two did not fully separate until 2001, when the Computer Laboratory moved out to the new William Gates building in <a href="/wiki/West_Cambridge" title="West Cambridge">West Cambridge</a>, off <a href="/wiki/Madingley_Road" title="Madingley Road">Madingley Road</a>, leaving behind an independent <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge_Computing_Service" title="University of Cambridge Computing Service">Computing Service</a>.</p>/n<p>In 2002, the Computer Laboratory launched the <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Computer_Lab_Ring" title="Cambridge Computer Lab Ring">Cambridge Computer Lab Ring</a>, a graduate society named after the <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Ring_(computer_network)" title="Cambridge Ring (computer network)">Cambridge Ring</a> network.</p>/n<p>On 30 June 2017, the <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_University_Reporter" title="Cambridge University Reporter">Cambridge University Reporter</a> announced that the Computer Laboratory would change its name to the Department of Computer Science and Technology from 1 October 2017, to reflect the broadened scope of its purpose and activities.<sup id="cite_ref-namechange_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-namechange-2">[2]</a></sup></p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current">Current</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_Laboratory,_University_of_Cambridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Current">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<p>The Computer Laboratory built and operated the world’s first fully operational practical <a href="/wiki/Stored_program" class="mw-redirect" title="Stored program">stored program</a> computer (<a href="/wiki/EDSAC" class="mw-redirect" title="EDSAC">EDSAC</a>, 1949) and offered the world’s first postgraduate taught course in computer science in 1953. It currently offers a 3-year undergraduate course and a 1-year masters course (with an optional natural language processing theme). Recent research has focused on <a href="/wiki/Platform_virtualization" class="mw-redirect" title="Platform virtualization">virtualization</a>, <a href="/wiki/Information_security" title="Information security">security</a>, <a href="/wiki/Usability" title="Usability">usability</a>, <a href="/wiki/Formal_verification" title="Formal verification">formal verification</a>, <a href="/wiki/Formal_semantics_of_programming_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Formal semantics of programming languages">formal semantics of programming languages</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_architecture" title="Computer architecture">computer architecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natural_language_processing" title="Natural language processing">natural language processing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wireless_networking" class="mw-redirect" title="Wireless networking">wireless networking</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biometric_identification" class="mw-redirect" title="Biometric identification">biometric identification</a>, <a href="/wiki/Routing" title="Routing">routing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Positioning_system" title="Positioning system">positioning systems</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a> (<i>“Computing for the future of the planet”</i>). Members of the Computer Laboratory have been involved in the creation of many successful UK <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">IT</a> companies such as <a href="/wiki/Acorn_Computers" title="Acorn Computers">Acorn</a>, <a href="/wiki/ARM_Limited" class="mw-redirect" title="ARM Limited">ARM</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=NCipher&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="NCipher (page does not exist)">nCipher</a> and <a href="/wiki/XenSource" class="mw-redirect" title="XenSource">XenSource</a>.</p>/n<p><br /></p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Staff">Staff</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_Laboratory,_University_of_Cambridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Staff">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Professors">Professors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_Laboratory,_University_of_Cambridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Professors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>/n<p>As of 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Computer_Laboratory,_University_of_Cambridge&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> the lab employs 19 Professors:<sup id="cite_ref-fortyacademicstaff_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fortyacademicstaff-3">[3]</a></sup></p>/n<div class="div-col columns column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 35em; -webkit-column-width: 35em; column-width: 35em;">/n<ol>/n<li><a href="/wiki/Ross_J._Anderson" title="Ross J.
