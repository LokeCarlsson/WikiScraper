colspan="2"></td>/n</tr>/n</table>/n<p><b>Martin Odersky</b> (born 5 September 1958) is a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> and professor of programming methods at <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_Polytechnique_F%C3%A9d%C3%A9rale_de_Lausanne" title="École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne">École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne</a> (EPFL) in Switzerland. He specializes in <a href="/wiki/Static_code_analysis" class="mw-redirect" title="Static code analysis">code analysis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Programming_language" title="Programming language">programming languages</a>. He designed the <a href="/wiki/Scala_(programming_language)" title="Scala (programming language)">Scala</a> programming language<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sjmc_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sjmc-3">[3]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Generic_Java" class="mw-redirect" title="Generic Java">Generic Java</a> (and <a href="/wiki/Pizza_(programming_language)" title="Pizza (programming language)">Pizza</a> before<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>) both with others. He implemented the GJ compiler <sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> ,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and his implementation became the basis of <a href="/wiki/Javac" title="Javac">javac</a>, the Java compiler.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2007, he was inducted as a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Fellows_of_the_Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="List of Fellows of the Association for Computing Machinery">Fellow of the Association for Computing Machinery</a>.</p>/n<p>In 1989, he received his Ph.D. from <a href="/wiki/ETH_Zurich" title="ETH Zurich">ETH Zurich</a> under the supervision of <a href="/wiki/Niklaus_Wirth" title="Niklaus Wirth">Niklaus Wirth</a>, who is best known as the designer of several programming languages, including <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(programming_language)" title="Pascal (programming language)">Pascal</a>. He did postdoctoral work at <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a>.</p>/n<p>On 12 May 2011, Odersky and collaborators launched Typesafe Inc. (renamed <a href="/wiki/Lightbend_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Lightbend Inc.">Lightbend Inc.</a>, February&#160;2016<span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="bday dtstart published updated">2016-02</span>)</span>), a company to provide commercial support, training, and services for Scala,<sup id="cite_ref-sjmc_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sjmc-3">[3]</a></sup> and he currently serves as the chairman and chief architect.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>/n<p>He teaches two courses on the <a href="/wiki/Massive_open_online_course" title="Massive open online course">massive open online course</a> (MOOC) provider, <a href="/wiki/Coursera" title="Coursera">Coursera</a>, namely <i>Functional Programming Principles in Scala</i> and <i>Functional Program Design in Scala</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Odersky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">/n<ol class="references">/n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://people.epfl.ch/martin.odersky/bio?lang=en&amp;cvlang=en">"Biographical notice on EPFL website"</a><span class="reference-accessdate">.
