title="Enlarge"></a></div>/nFU-Fighters, World Champion 2004, 2005: Artificial intelligence football robots created by the team around Raúl Rojas.</div>/n</div>/n</div>/n<p><b>Raúl Rojas González</b> (born 1955, in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>) is a professor of Computer Science and Mathematics at the <a href="/wiki/Free_University_of_Berlin" title="Free University of Berlin">Free University of Berlin</a> and a renowned specialist in <a href="/wiki/Artificial_neural_networks" class="mw-redirect" title="Artificial neural networks">artificial neural networks</a>. The FU-Fighters, <a href="/wiki/RoboCup" title="RoboCup">football-playing robots</a> he helped build, were world champions in 2004 and 2005. He is now leading an <a href="/wiki/Driverless_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Driverless car">autonomous car</a> project called Spirit of Berlin.</p>/n<p>He and his team were awarded the <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_von_Kempelen" title="Wolfgang von Kempelen">Wolfgang von Kempelen</a> Prize for his work on <a href="/wiki/Konrad_Zuse" title="Konrad Zuse">Konrad Zuse</a> and the history of <a href="/wiki/Computers" class="mw-redirect" title="Computers">computers</a>. Although most of his current research and teaching revolves around <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a> and its applications, he holds academic degrees in mathematics and economics.</p>/n<p>In 2009 the Mexican government created the Raúl Rojas González Prize for scientific achievement by Mexican citizens. The first recipient of the prize was Luis Rafael Herrera Estrella, for contributions to plant biotechnology.</p>/n<p>He ran for president at the <a href="/wiki/Free_University_of_Berlin" title="Free University of Berlin">Free University of Berlin</a> in 2010. <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>/n<p></p>/n<div id="toc" class="toc">/n<div class="toctitle">/n<h2>Contents</h2>/n</div>/n<ul>/n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>/n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Prizes"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Prizes</span></a></li>/n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Works"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Works</span></a></li>/n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>/n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>/n</ul>/n</div>/n<p></p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Rojas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<p>Raúl Rojas was born on June 25th, 1955 in Mexico City to an engineer and a teacher. He attended university at the <a href="/wiki/National_Polytechnic_Institute" class="mw-redirect" title="National Polytechnic Institute">National Polytechnic Institute</a> in Mexico City, where he majored in Mathematics and Physics. He moved to Germany in 1982 as a doctoral student in economics under the guidance of the political economist <a href="/wiki/Elmar_Altvater" title="Elmar Altvater">Elmar Altvater</a>. The resulting dissertation was published under the title "Die Armut der Nationen - Handbuch zur Schuldenkrise von Argentinien bis Zaire" (The poverty of nations - Handbook of debt crisis from Argentina to Zaire). He became a full professor at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Halle-Wittenberg" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Halle-Wittenberg">University of Halle-Wittenberg</a> in 1994, and later moved to the <a href="/wiki/Free_University_of_Berlin" title="Free University of Berlin">Free University of Berlin</a>, where he remains today in the Informatics department. His wife, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Margarita_Esponda_Arg%C3%BCero&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Margarita Esponda Argüero (page does not exist)">Margarita Esponda Argüero</a>, is a professor in the same department.</p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prizes">Prizes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Rojas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Prizes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<ul>/n<li>2001: Gründerpreis Multimedia of the German Ministry of Finance and Technology</li>/n<li>2002: European Academic Software Award</li>/n<li>2004 and 2005: World champions in football robots with the FU-Fighters</li>/n<li>2005: Wolfgang von Kempelen Prize for the History of Informatics</li>/n<li>2009: Received the Heberto Castillo gold medal for contributions to science from the Mexico City government</li>/n<li>2015: Was named Professor of the year by the Association of German Universities</li>/n<li>2015: Received the National Prize of Sciences and Arts by the Mexican Government in the category of Technology and Design</li>/n</ul>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Works">Works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Rojas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<ul>/n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://page.mi.fu-berlin.de/rojas/neural/index.html"><i>Neural Networks - A Systematic Introduction</i></a> Springer, Berlin, 1996.
