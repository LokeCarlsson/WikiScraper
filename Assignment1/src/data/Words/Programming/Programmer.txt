The term <i>computer programmer</i> can refer to a specialist in one area of <a href="/wiki/Computer_programming" title="Computer programming">computer programming</a> or to a generalist who writes code for many kinds of software. One who practices or professes a formal approach to programming may also be known as a <b>programmer analyst</b>.</p>/n<p>A programmer's primary <a href="/wiki/Computer_language" title="Computer language">computer language</a> (<a href="/wiki/Assembly_language" title="Assembly language">Assembly</a>, <a href="/wiki/COBOL" title="COBOL">COBOL</a>, <a href="/wiki/C_(programming_language)" title="C (programming language)">C</a>, <a href="/wiki/C%2B%2B" title="C++">C++</a>, <a href="/wiki/C_Sharp_(programming_language)" title="C Sharp (programming language)">C#</a>, <a href="/wiki/Java_(programming_language)" title="Java (programming language)">Java</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lisp_(programming_language)" title="Lisp (programming language)">Lisp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Python_programming_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Python programming language">Python</a>, etc.) is often prefixed to these titles, and those who work in a <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">web</a> environment often prefix their titles with <i>web</i>.</p>/n<p>A range of occupations, including: <a href="/wiki/Software_developer" title="Software developer">software developer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Web_developer" title="Web developer">web developer</a>, mobile applications developer, embedded <a href="/wiki/Firmware" title="Firmware">firmware</a> developer, <a href="/wiki/Software_engineer" title="Software engineer">software engineer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Software_analyst" title="Software analyst">software analyst</a>, while they do involve programming, also require a range of other skills. class="thumbcaption">/n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ada_Lovelace_portrait.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>/n<a href="/wiki/Ada_Lovelace" title="Ada Lovelace">Ada Lovelace</a> is considered by many as the first computer programmer.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></div>/n</div>/n</div>/n<p>British countess and mathematician <a href="/wiki/Ada_Lovelace" title="Ada Lovelace">Ada Lovelace</a> is often considered the first computer programmer, as she was the first to publish an <a href="/wiki/Algorithm" title="Algorithm">algorithm</a> intended for implementation on <a href="/wiki/Charles_Babbage" title="Charles Babbage">Charles Babbage</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Analytical_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Analytical engine">analytical engine</a>, in October 1842, intended for the calculation of <a href="/wiki/Bernoulli_number" title="Bernoulli number">Bernoulli numbers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Because Babbage's machine was never completed to a functioning standard in her time, she never saw this algorithm run.</p>/n<p>The first person to run a program on a functioning modern electronically based computer was <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> <a href="/wiki/Konrad_Zuse" title="Konrad Zuse">Konrad Zuse</a>, in 1941.</p>/n<p>The <a href="/wiki/ENIAC" title="ENIAC">ENIAC</a> programming team, consisting of <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Antonelli" title="Kathleen Antonelli">Kay McNulty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Bartik" title="Jean Bartik">Betty Jennings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Betty_Holberton" title="Betty Holberton">Betty Snyder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marlyn_Meltzer" title="Marlyn Meltzer">Marlyn Wescoff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frances_Spence" title="Frances Spence">Fran Bilas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ruth_Teitelbaum" title="Ruth Teitelbaum">Ruth Lichterman</a> were the first regularly working programmers.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>/n<p>International Programmers' Day is celebrated annually on 7 January.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2009, the title="Programmers' Day">Programmers' Day</a> to be celebrated on 13 September (12 September in leap years). It had also been an unofficial international holiday before that..</p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nature_of_the_work">Nature of the work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Programmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Nature of the work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>/n<dl>/n<dd><i>Some of this section is from the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.bls.gov/oco/ocos110.htm">Occupational Outlook Handbook</a>, 2006–07 Edition, which is in the <a href="/wiki/Public_domain" title="Public domain">public domain</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Work_of_the_United_States_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Work of the United States Government">work of the United States Government</a>.</i></dd>/n</dl>/n<p>Computer programmers write, test, <a href="/wiki/Debug" class="mw-redirect" title="Debug">debug</a>, and maintain the detailed instructions, called <a href="/wiki/Computer_programs" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer programs">computer programs</a>, that computers must follow to perform their functions. Programmers also conceive, design, and test logical structures for solving problems by computer. Many technical innovations in programming&#160;— advanced computing technologies and sophisticated new languages and programming tools&#160;— have redefined the role of a programmer and elevated much of the programming work done today. Job titles and descriptions may vary, depending on the organization.</p>/n<p>Programmers work in many settings, including corporate <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a> ("IT") departments, big <a href="/wiki/Software_companies" class="mw-redirect" title="Software companies">software companies</a>, small service firms and government entities of all sizes. Many professional programmers also work for consulting companies at client sites as <a href="/wiki/Independent_contractor" title="Independent contractor">contractors</a>. <a href="/wiki/License" title="License">Licensing</a> is not typically required to work as a programmer, although <a href="/wiki/Professional_certification" title="Professional certification">professional certifications</a> are commonly held by programmers. Programming is widely considered a <a href="/wiki/Profession" title="Profession">profession</a> (although some<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (March 2011)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> authorities disagree on the grounds that only careers with legal licensing requirements count as a profession).</p>/n<p>Programmers' work varies widely depending on the type of business for which they are writing programs. For example, the instructions involved in updating financial records are very different from those required to duplicate conditions on an aircraft for pilots training in a flight simulator. Simple programs can be written in a few hours, more complex ones may require more than a year of work, while others are never considered 'complete' but rather are continuously improved as long as they stay in use. In most cases, several programmers work together as a team under a senior programmer’s supervision.</p>/n<div class="thumb tright">/n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:227px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Programmer_writing_code_with_Unit_Tests.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Programmer_writing_code_with_Unit_Tests.jpg/225px-Programmer_writing_code_with_Unit_Tests.jpg" width="225" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Programmer_writing_code_with_Unit_Tests.jpg/338px-Programmer_writing_code_with_Unit_Tests.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Programmer_writing_code_with_Unit_Tests.jpg/450px-Programmer_writing_code_with_Unit_Tests.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1404" data-file-height="936" /></a>/n<div class="thumbcaption">/n<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Programmer_writing_code_with_Unit_Tests.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>/nA computer programmer editing the <a href="/wiki/Source_code" title="Source code">source code</a> of a piece of <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a>.</div>/n</div>/n</div>/n<p>Programmers write programs according to the specifications determined primarily by more senior programmers and by <a href="/wiki/Systems_analyst" title="Systems analyst">systems analysts</a>. After the design process is complete, it is the job of the programmer to convert that design into a logical series of instructions that the computer can follow. The programmer codes these instructions in one of many programming languages. Different programming languages are used depending on the purpose of the program. <a href="/wiki/COBOL" title="COBOL">COBOL</a>, for example, is commonly used for business applications that typically run on <a href="/wiki/Mainframe_computer" title="Mainframe computer">mainframe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minicomputer" title="Minicomputer">midrange</a> computers, whereas <a href="/wiki/Fortran" title="Fortran">Fortran</a> is used in science and engineering. <a href="/wiki/C%2B%2B" title="C++">C++</a> is widely used for both scientific and business applications. <a href="/wiki/Java_(programming_language)" title="Java (programming language)">Java</a>, <a href="/wiki/C_Sharp_(programming_language)" title="C Sharp (programming language)">C#</a>, <a href="/wiki/Visual_Basic" title="Visual Basic">VB</a> and <a href="/wiki/PHP" title="PHP">PHP</a> are popular programming languages for Web and business applications. Programmers generally know more than one programming language and, because many languages are similar, they often can learn new languages relatively easily. In practice, programmers often are referred to by the language they know, e.g. as <i>Java programmers</i>, or by the type of function they perform or environment in which they work: for example, <i><a href="/wiki/Database" title="Database">database</a> programmers</i>, <i>mainframe programmers</i>, or <a href="/wiki/Web_developer" title="Web developer">Web developers</a>.</p>/n<p>When making changes to the <a href="/wiki/Source_code" title="Source code">source code</a> that programs are made up of, programmers need to make other programmers aware of the task that the routine is to perform. They do this by inserting comments in the <a href="/wiki/Source_code" title="Source code">source code</a> so that others can understand the program more easily and by <a href="/wiki/Software_documentation" title="Software documentation">documenting their code</a>. To save work, programmers often use <a href="/wiki/Library_(computing)" title="Library (computing)">libraries</a> of basic code that can be modified or customized for a specific application. This approach yields more reliable and consistent programs and increases programmers' productivity by eliminating some routine steps.</p>/n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Testing_and_debugging">Testing and debugging</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Programmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Testing and debugging">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>/n<p>Programmers test a program by running it and looking for <a href="/wiki/Software_bug" title="Software bug">bugs</a> (errors). As they are identified, the programmer usually makes the appropriate corrections, then rechecks the program until an acceptably low level and severity of bugs remain. This process is called <a href="/wiki/Software_testing" title="Software testing">testing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Debugging" title="Debugging">debugging</a>. These are important parts of every programmer's job. Programmers may continue to fix these problems throughout the life of a program. Updating, repairing, modifying, and expanding existing programs is sometimes called <i>maintenance programming</i>. Programmers may contribute to <a href="/wiki/User_guide" title="User guide">user guides</a> and <a href="/wiki/Online_help" title="Online help">online help</a>, or they may work with <a href="/wiki/Technical_writer" title="Technical writer">technical writers</a> to do such work.</p>/n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Application_versus_system_programming">Application versus system programming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Programmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Application versus system programming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>/n<p>Computer programmers often are grouped into two broad types: application programmers and systems programmers. Application programmers write programs to handle a specific job, such as a program to track inventory within an organization. They also may revise existing packaged software or customize generic applications which are frequently purchased from <a href="/wiki/Independent_software_vendor" title="Independent software vendor">independent software vendors</a>. Systems programmers, in contrast, write programs to maintain and control computer systems software, such as <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating systems</a> and <a href="/wiki/Database_management_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Database management system">database management systems</a>. These workers make changes in the instructions that determine how the network, workstations, and <a href="/wiki/Central_processing_unit" title="Central processing unit">CPU</a> of the system handle the various jobs they have been given and how they communicate with peripheral equipment such as <a href="/wiki/Computer_printer" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer printer">printers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Data_storage_device" class="mw-redirect" title="Data storage device">disk drives</a>.</p>/n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_software">Types of software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Programmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Types of software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>/n<p>Programmers in software development companies may work directly with experts from various fields to create software&#160;– either programs designed for specific clients or packaged software for general use&#160;– ranging from <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video games</a> to educational software to programs for <a href="/wiki/Desktop_publishing" title="Desktop publishing">desktop publishing</a> and financial planning. Programming of packaged software constitutes one of the most rapidly growing segments of the computer services industry. Some companies or organizations – even small ones – have set up their own IT team to ensure the design and development of in-house software to answer to very specific needs from their internal end-users, especially when existing software are not suitable or too expensive. This is for example the case in <a href="/wiki/Research_laboratories" class="mw-redirect" title="Research laboratories">research laboratories</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>/n<p>In some organizations, particularly small ones, people commonly known as <i>programmer analysts</i> are responsible for both the systems analysis and the actual programming work. The transition from a mainframe environment to one that is based primarily on <a href="/wiki/Personal_computers" class="mw-redirect" title="Personal computers">personal computers</a> (PCs) has blurred the once rigid distinction between the programmer and the user. Increasingly, adept end users are taking over many of the tasks previously performed by programmers. For example, the growing use of packaged software, such as spreadsheet and database management software packages, allows users to write simple programs to access data and perform calculations.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>/n<p>In addition, the rise of the Internet has made <a href="/wiki/Web_development" title="Web development">web development</a> a huge part of the programming field. Currently more software applications are <a href="/wiki/Web_application" title="Web application">web applications</a> that can be used by anyone with a <a href="/wiki/Web_browser" title="Web browser">web browser</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Examples of such applications include the <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> search service, the <a href="/wiki/Outlook.com" title="Outlook.com">Outlook.com</a> e-mail service, and the <a href="/wiki/Flickr" title="Flickr">Flickr</a> photo-sharing service.</p>/n<p>Programming editors, also known as <a href="/wiki/Source_code_editor" title="Source code editor">source code editors</a>, are text editors that are specifically designed for programmers or developers for writing the source code of an application or a program. Most of these editors include features useful for programmers, which may include color <a href="/wiki/Syntax_highlighting" title="Syntax highlighting">syntax highlighting</a>, auto indentation, <a href="/wiki/Auto-complete" class="mw-redirect" title="Auto-complete">auto-complete</a>, bracket matching, <a href="/wiki/Syntax_checker" class="mw-redirect" title="Syntax checker">syntax check</a>, and allows <a href="/wiki/Plug-in_(computing)" title="Plug-in (computing)">plug-ins</a>. These features aid the users during coding, <a href="/wiki/Debugging" title="Debugging">debugging</a> and testing.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>/n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Globalization">Globalization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a Programming was even mentioned in the 2004 US Presidential debate on the topic of <a href="/wiki/Offshore_outsourcing" title="Offshore outsourcing">offshore outsourcing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>/n<p>Large companies claim there is a <a href="/wiki/Skills_shortage" class="mw-redirect" title="Skills shortage">skills shortage</a> with regard to programming talent. However, US programmers and unions counter that large companies are exaggerating their case in order to obtain cheaper programmers from developing countries and avoid previously employer paid training using industry specific technologies not covered in most accredited degree programs.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Other reasons for employers claiming skill shortages is the result of their own cost saving combining of several disparate skill sets previously held by several specialized programmers into fewer generalized <a href="/wiki/Mechatronics" title="Mechatronics">multifaceted</a> positions that are unlikely to have enough <a href="/wiki/Purple_squirrel" title="Purple squirrel">"qualified" candidates</a> with the desired experience.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>/n<p>Enrollment in computer-related degrees in US has dropped recently due to lack of general interests in science and mathematics and also out of an apparent fear that programming will be subject to the same pressures as manufacturing and agriculture careers.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> This situation has resulted in confusion about whether the US economy is entering a "post-<a href="/wiki/Quaternary_sector_of_the_economy" title="Quaternary sector of the economy">information</a> age" and the nature of US <a href="/wiki/Comparative_advantage" title="Comparative advantage">comparative advantages</a>. Most academic institutions have an <a href="/wiki/Institutional_research" title="Institutional research">Institutional research</a> office that keep past statistics of degrees conferred which show several dips and rises in Computer Science degrees over the past 30 years. The overall trend shows a slightly overall decline in growth (especially when compared to other STEM degree growth) since certain peaks of <a href="/wiki/Early_1980s_recession_in_the_United_States" title="Early 1980s recession in the United States">1986</a>, <a href="/wiki/Early_1990s_recession_in_the_United_States" title="Early 1990s recession in the United States">1992</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dot-com_bubble" title="Dot-com bubble">2002</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Great_Recession" title="Great Recession">2008</a> showing periods of flat growth or even declines<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>. In addition the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics Occupational Outlook 2014-24 is -8% (a decline in their words) for Computer Programmers because Computer programming can be done from anywhere in the
